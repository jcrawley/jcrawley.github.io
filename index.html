Our Dreams:

A Plan for the future

By: Stefan Dyroff, Jen Bullock, Joe Crawley

What is Our Dreams?

Founded in October 2013

Non-profit 501(c) equivalent in the Philippines

Board of Directors all cousins in the “Constantino” family

Provide high school scholarships and promote STE(A)M education

Roadblocks?

No source of sustainble funding

No plan for future funding

Concept

New entreprenuers need developers to make their products but may not know what they need technology-wise to attract developers

There are many software developers in the Philippines that work for a low salary

New Entreprenuers receive specifics and labor on what they need for ideas

Filipino programmers receive work, higher wages, and more secure job history, all while supporting education for Filipino youth

How it works

We and programmers determine what is necessary for project

We retain freelance coders and determine which coder(s) are needed for the project

Marketing

Promotions at college campuses, entrepreneur conventions

Small online targeted ad campaign(Google)

Financial Analysis

Goal

Average coder salary in Philippines

$500/year x 10 scholarships (goal) = $5000

$220/mo. / 150 hours = $1.47

We will pay $4 / hour and charge $20 / hour= $16 profit / hour

$5000 / $16 = 313 hours to reach our goal.

Analysis of Opportunity

Competition

Many other companies are using this model. Still, our model allows for potentially lower prices as well as a true social benefit to Filipino coders and students alike.

Key Competitors

oDesk.com, elance.com, craigslist.com

Pros

lower cost to entrepreneur, social benefit/story

Cons

competition, two-sided, tough to get going, difficult to organize network of coders

Risks

costs of startup, too much competition
